This will be a 5-room dungeon run as one of the trials for the Winter Trials. The tone will be a simple D&D 5-room dungeon, centered around the trolls as a main minion, but with the twist of two parties attempting to clear the dungeon at the same time.

**Gruel the Rotten** is a Troll who has significant power through his exorbitant feast. His powerful magic, brutal strength, and militia of trolls pose a powerful threat to any who stand in his way.

The party is placed before the tower entrance, eventually brings them to the caves of Gruel's Lair. This encounter has them squared off against the Golden Rings, where they also are attempting to clear this dungeon and recover the treasure held by Gruel.
### Time
An important thing about a dungeon race is speed. You have a set of puzzles you have to accomplish faster than the other party. Both real-time with party decision making and in-game time with the option dictated by the party will be added up into a time-score. This score will judge how favorable the situation the party will have in terms of time when both parties reach the final dungeon. 

### The Entrance
A grand illusion veils across the arena, transforming your environment entirely. You find yourselves within the deep neck of a mildly swampy coniferous forest, along a lightly-walked trail. The trees tower over you, but the blue sky and light from the sun peaks through. Before you, a large tower stands a few hundred feet away. 
## Room 1: The Guardian Puzzle
Two Riverlands Moss-Troll seems to be patrolling the front entrance. Upon each of the troll's belts sits something that seems to pulsate between the fabric of the bag it is stored. 
- The pulsating item is a Fruit of the River-Flower. The River-Flower is a legendary plant, and [[Natural Spirit]]. This plant grows a fruit every day that holds immense power, allowing for great healing and rejuvenation of resources. If a creature eats this flower, they may regain 4d8+4 HP or up to 2 levels in spell-slots. 

The trolls both stand within a circle that is dug into the ground around the tower, maybe 40ft in radius, but only about 15ft away from the tower. A
- This circle is designed as an Alarm. Any creature not allowed into the tower will be alerted to the Troll Shaman who created the circle. This discourages bypassing the puzzle via stealth.
- A successful arcana check of DC 15 can detect that the trigger is bypassing it. A DC 20 will detect that it is an esoteric casting of alarm.

**The Tower Entrance** is a massive and intricate stone door. In the center, there seems to two fixtures that can hold an object.
- Placing the Fruit of the River-Flower in each fixture will cause the door to glow and unlock. The fruits will be drained, being unusable after.
- The door can be broken with around 4 levels of spells slots.

## Room 2: The Pressure Plate
At the end of the room, you see the foot of a large set of spiral stairs that split across each wall.

Before the stairs, you the entire floor seems to be a pressure plate, and holes in the wall next to it with a red stain all over the floor and around the holes.

At the top of each set of stairs, a locked door. The doors cannot be opened, without the use of a powerful spell-slot or by stepping on the pressure plate. The staircase leading left goes to room 3 and the staircase right goes to room 4.
