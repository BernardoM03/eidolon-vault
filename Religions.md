Religions are a framework that organizes one's life, often including **belief in a higher power** or **shared beliefs** and **actions**. 
- The Abrahamic religions are an example of religions that do all three. They believe in God, have a shared set of beliefs such as "Jesus is our savior," and take action in worship such as chastity.
- Buddhism does not believe in a higher power, but has a shared set of beliefs in the purpose of self-enlightenment and actions of meditation and separating oneself from material possessions.

Religions as an identity only developed in the turn to modernity within Europe. Religion now, can be a private matter, separate from the rest of life. In pre-modern societies, religion was just a "part of life."
