## Wall Town 
The Black Market is named "Wall Town", a small section of Dimming. This is a cultural hub, consistently packed with the criminals, low-lives, and degenerates of the city. Those who spend their evenings in the Promenade move to spend their night in Gutter. Wealth is flaunted and modesty is discouraged. 

## Halandar and LaVey
You continue down the steps from the trap door, reaching an old and slightly rotted wooden door. You emerge into a small shop, roughly 10ft by 10ft. Everything seemed to be draped in clothes, covered in dust, with spider webs covering. A taken down sign rests against a wall. "Halandar and LaVey." A old shop, seemingly abandoned now. 

If the party wishes to investigate, they can find a few items of note on different DC checks
- DC 5: Dusty shelves carry brittle tomes written in fading inks, many bound in cracked leather. Several skulls sit among the displays. Some are clearly animal, others possibly humanoid, though age has stripped them of obvious features. Most of the books appear mundane: old genealogies, outdated occult pamphlets, travel logs, and collections of poorly copied rituals that likely never worked.
- DC 10: A closer look reveals that many of the books and curios share themes related to necromancy and diabolism. Despite that, the actual items are disappointingly harmless.
- DC 15: This collection of necromantic and diabolic artifacts seems to be quite rare indeed. Nothing is particular powerful or dangerous, but a lot of books here are ones that are heavily studied and well researched. Whoever owned this shop did their due diligence when it comes to the research of these dangerous topics. 
- DC 20: Mounted alone in a velvet-lined glass casing sits a single object. A glassy pearl swirls with blackened arcane energy. With an arcana check, you can feel the power of the Shadowfell run through it. 
- DC 25: A letter between Oswyn LaVey and a man named Baeldric Vauldra. The letter denotes Baeldric Vauldra's identity as a devil. [[Baato's Letter to Oswyn LaVey]]

**Magic Items:** 
- Pearl of Shadow (Uncommon Wondrous Item): This pearl contains an essence of the Shadowfell. Once you smash the pearl, it's energy is released, clinging to nearby sources of Identity. For the next 2d4 turns, you and a number of creatures within 5 feet of the smashed pearl gain the "Invisible condition".

Exiting Halandar and LaVey, the outside of the shop is just as dark and cramped as inside. On the walls, posters are aged, ripped, and smeared. Most of them seem illegible. In the distance you hear the muffled sounds of ringing, shouts, and music down the underground alleyway you find yourself in. 
## Entering Wall Town
Walking down the alleyway, the lights and music of this entertainment quarter begin to show itself. The scent of strong liquor, pipe smoke, and spiced street food hangs thick in the air. Laughter erupts from crowded doorways, dice rattle across wooden tables, and somewhere deeper within the district a stringed instrument struggles to be heard over drunken singing.

You begin to pass crowds of people, dressed lavishly. Velvet coats, glistening jewelry, unbuttoned shirts, dresses cut far bolder that polite society would ever consider above ground. How do you take in this environment

### The Wall
The most famous part of Wall Town is the titular wall that faces each of the stores and stalls of the market. The wall is covered in large wanted posters, signs, advertisements, and other messages from Wall Town goers. Certain sections of the wall are carved out specifically for certain people or factions. 

### Manse de Mortem
A large and incredibly gaudy mansion owned by the [[Guilds & Groups/Gravel Rats|Gravel Rats]] Capo, Fortuna Mortem. This mansion was recently taken over by the Gravel Rats as a show of force and authority over the past few years. This mansion is heavily guarded by Gravel Rat Enforcers. NONE are allowed in. If exploring, please see [[Manse de Mortem]]

### Announcing the New Bounties.
A loud burst of music begins to play from the other side of the plaza. The sound originates from the [[Manse de Mortem]], as a Fortuna Mortem exits in a parade of praise. A large velvet red carpet is being rolled out by two young children, one boy and one girl, who seem to be working as fast and hard as they can. The parade continues for a shocking long period of time.

As the music begins to die down, Fortuna riles the crowd up in excitement. They seem to all participate in this event, knowing what's about to occur. It is the ceremony to announce the new bounties.
