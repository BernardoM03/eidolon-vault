## Recap
Last session, you started in the office of Vurn Orleth, Grand Seneschal of Catameria. Discussing plans regarding the situation, your meeting was interrupted by an older tiefling man, with a tiger-striped cloak and fine clothes, Baeldric Vauldra. His playfulness and ego seemed to offset everyone except for Jaspar, who offered to have a drink sometime. 

Following, you left the office and had lunch with Lucky's dad at a restaurant in the Merial Docks. While enjoying the meal, you planned to find information about what House Meredane was hiding. Lucky and Cogsworth, you snuck onto a boat following the "agent" you met at South-Point Lighthouse. Cogsworth, you continued to tail him and Lucky you waited within the cargo-hold to find out what was being shipped. Cogsworth, you tailed him but not well enough to do so without alerting his attention. After a tense few minutes, you call him over and attempt to cause a distraction to kidnap him. Instead, he starts the barfight and attempts to escape, but gets an ANCHOR dropped on him. You ALL get kicked out, but successfully captured the agent, bringing him home to interrogate more information out of him. 

Last we left off, you all ready for a long-rest decided to sleep at Brennly's House. Just before the time for sleep, some of you could hear the shattering of glass. Lucky and Twila, I believe, you decided to see what was happening outside. The orange light was first apparent, followed by the acrid smell of Alchemist's Fire burning upon your roof. 

Partially illuminated by the flames, were the shining and oily black feathers of Edward Crowley, A Gravel Rat captain and the chief dealer in magical artifacts and contraband. In his annoyingly nasally voice, he issued commands to five other Aarakocra. "Spread fire across the roof. Drive them out into the horde or trap them in with the flames." 

Inside the building, the glass windows shatter inwards as rocks are pelted into the foyer. Jaspar, you see climbing from the window, the familiar brutish arm of [[Renk the Sorry]] as he claims his "master was not very happy with what happened today." And in that moment, he disappeared.

### Gravel Rat Bandit Groups
Stay within positions. Focus fire anyone who attempts to leave through the windows. They will directly follow orders from Crowley, but disperse once 4/6 members die.
- +4 to hit, n attacks for each rat in group, 1d8+2 piercing
- AC 14 (Disadvantage to hit because dim light, hiding), Rat dies if they take more than 6 dmg.
- AoE attacks have potential to over cap damage, killing 1 for each instance of 6 dmg.

### Edward Crowley
Stays airborne and directs his forces with his keen vision and battle tactics. Doesn't attack until it is required of him. He will only engage in melee when the conditions are ideal.
1. Target is able to be taken down in a swift turn OR there is a numerical advantage
2. A target is downed. He will order two murderers to follow him, and will retrieve the body.
Crowley will retreat if he approaches 50% HP. If 3+ of his Murderer's die, he will also call off the mission, but in Auran. He will leave the bandits as a distraction. This will be considered defeat. 
- +9 to hit, 2 attacks with 1d6+6 each. 1x charge for additional 2d6 lightning.
- 3 Charges: 30ft teleport with DC14 DEX dealing 8d6 to those in line
- AC 17, 84 HP, 60ft flying

### Renk the Sorry
Stays invisible as constantly as possible. Will threaten the party to get more information about "the original design." He will offer to "kill the pests" for the party as a show of good faith. There is a bit of a puzzle in the language barrier here that the party must decipher. Once they know what he wants, the party can negotiate and easily convince him to join their side.
- +9 to hit, 2 attacks with 1d12+6 + 1d8 necrotic claw or 2d6+2 with Frighten ray.
- AC 14, 127 HP, 30ft movement, Invisibility as a bonus action

## Post Combat 
Combat is now over, for the most part. No more initiative, no more Gravel Rats. The enemies have dispersed, but they do see one figure down the street. The halfling figure of Brennly Wriggle.
### Brennly's Return
Brennly, now after his rest and travel back, has made it home to Catameria. He strolls home bag over one shoulder and a wrapped pastry in hand, only to find his estate has been reduced to rubble. 

He is devastated, not crying but the emotion is evident on his face. He seems lost in thought, but looks to see you all. He see's the harm, and worry washes your face as you watch him scan for Atar, Cogsworth, and the others he met in the village. "Oh good. You're all still in one piece." His sadness is immediately replaced by relief. And he also greets the others.

He will not let them apologize first. He gets there before them.

"I know what happened in the valley. Chiryl wrote to me." He turns his hat over in his hands. "I should have told you what I knew before any of this started. I thought I was being careful. I thought I had it contained." A pause. "I am very sorry. For all of it."

"Now. Someone tell me everything, because I've been on a cart for near two weeks with nothing but my own thoughts. I had thought the worst."

## The Stone Crown and Meredane Manor
You climb through the north-western roads. The walk is long and mostly straight, seemingly made for carriages. Before you lies the Stone Crown, a sight that most of you have only ever seen from the city below. It is enormous, shaped of dark slate stone that seems to blend into the night sky. Five large palaces are built into the base, serving as the homes for the five great noble houses: Bellgrane, Meredane, Tesseron, Thornvale, and Valcaryn. Climbing even higher is the Castella Merion, home to the Royal Family and Throne to the King of Catameria. [Roleplay moment here]

Up here, the air is colder and the noise of the city below falls away faster than it should. By the time the gate comes into view, the city below looks like someone else's problem. "Around here" Renk whispers, ushering you in the direction of a off-path trail that wraps beneath the Crown, avoiding the main gate where guards seem to be standing. After a few minutes, Renk stops and examines the Wall. The spell obscuring you drops, and he begins to utter an incantation. A small pathway opens, a small staircase going up. "You first peasant trashes, Renk must close the path." [roleplay moment here]

You emerge above the stone, into a small garden maze. It's nothing complicated to traverse, and quite quaint Navigating your way through the beautifully maintained baskets of peonies and chrysanthemums, seemingly favorites of the house, you emerge towards an entrance to a large palace. The stonework is the same dark basalt as the ridge it sits on. At the center of the door, cast in iron, is a flying fish mid-leap. The Meredane Estate. Renk disappears beside you. "Renk will meet peasant trashes inside. Renk sees Young Master now" [roleplay moment here]

The door swings open, revealing a dark-haired woman wearing a dark-sack suit above a well-fitting dress. Her tone is simple and comforting. "Welcome Thunder-Cats, to the home of House Meredane. If you have any questions, my name is Karina. Otherwise, please follow me." [roleplay moment here]

Inside, the ceilings are tall and the light is low and warm and the walls are a similar black slate, but polished and ornately decorated. The floor seems to be tiled with a pale marble, intermittently covered by massive rugs of various type. Karina speaks as you walk through this entrance hall. "On either side of the hall, shelves and cabinets display the history of House Meredane's vast and long-standing trading empire. Spice jars from the Balatan plains, furs from creatures from the island of Abenasu, and currencies from every known city of Ennalis to state the few," she states pointing to different objects on the shelves. To those of you with an appreciation for the artisanal, this is expected but incredibly impressive nonetheless. [roleplay moment here]

A door to your left sits slightly ajar. Through the gap, you see an older man, tan skin and dark-brown hair, who sits at the head of a long table, speaking in a low measured voice to two officials whose backs are to you. He glances up to see you pass for a second, but continues the conversation. "Please excuse his Lordship for not being able to greet you. He is busy with an important conversation, but I am sure he would have met with if you if that were not the case." She is referring to Marek Meredane, the head of the House. [roleplay moment here]

Karina leads you to up to the second floor, past portraits of Meredane's going back far enough that the fashion becomes unrecognizable. As you climb the stairs, you see a figure behind the railing of the third floor. This young man has light-brown hair and bears a striking resemblance to one of the portraits you passed by. He seems to be lost in thought, staring at a coin in his hand. After a moment, he looks down at you, and immediately turns around and walks. Jaspar you now recognize this as the man you met in the alleyway.

You continue down the second floor, and Karina opens a room that is large, quiet, and comfortable. Two beds and a sofa. A window overlooking the rear garden. A fireplace already lit. She sets a key on the side table. "The family asks that you remain as guests of the house this evening. Breakfast is available from first light. Should you need anything before then, pull the cord by the door." She closes the door behind her, leaving you to yourselves.

The only sounds are of the winds, a faint batting of wings, and the occasional thump of the rooftops. There is nowhere to hide here, only places to look like you are walking with purpose. Your cloaks work to hide your general figure, as you occasionally pass by groups of delinquents you believe are searching for you.
