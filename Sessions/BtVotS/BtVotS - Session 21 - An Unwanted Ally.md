## Helena and the Tower of Abjuration
Helena's tower resides in the Verdure District, near the spires of Amerderth in a ward of mages. Some of the most powerful arcanists through out the world live here, striving with their  research and magic development to stay at the foremost of this field. Being a nation of peace has allowed the variation of magic to flourish, but the urgency cannot match the militarized semi-magocracy of Balatan Empire.
### Outside
This building is simple yet elegant, just like Helena's practice of Abjuration. The towers elects to be short, with minimal windows and entrances. The stone is a slate gray, and the door is of sturdy oak. A small garden remains outside, currently being maintained by a small number of tiny fey. As you approach, you sense a small shimmer before you, the first protective barrier surrounding this building, at least that you noticed.
### Inside
Inside, the building is as you remember Twila. Tidy and organized. The main room opens up to a small living space, mostly covered with bookshelves and scroll-cubbies. A fireplace sits next to a satin chair, a small yet fluffy grey kitten is asleep. 
- This was adopted shortly after Twila left, with Helena feeling lonely by not knowing how to deal with it. His name is Elminster.
### A Rakshasa's Weakness
Helena, while willing to help, is deeply unimpressed with the party's ability to make enemies with a "Rakshasa." She will reiterate their strengths, and how unfortunate a match up it is.
- "Twila, dear." She puts her hand to her forehead, rubbing the temples as if your words resparked a migraine. "This is quite possibly the worst enemy you could have made."
- "Well, I'm sure you might know this but those devils are immune to targeted magic, irrelevant of it's nature. Perhaps a large blast of magic, such as a Fireball could deal some damage, but not nearly for it's worth in energy. The only recorded strategy to defeat a devil such as that is to overpower them physically, preferably with blessed stabbing weapon. In addition, their deaths are never permanent. You'd have to rewrite his soul completely, which can only be done with access to the [[Grey King's Codex]], and unless you have personal access to the Merion Treasury, that would be impossible."

### The Grey King's Codex
A tome crafted by the Grey King himself. Whatever is declared within its pages becomes the new truth of the subject it names, overriding whatever existed before. But this does not come without deeply traumatic side effects.
