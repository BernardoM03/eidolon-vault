### Prev Session Summary
The party makes their way down to city proper, the Colosseum, to register themselves into the Winter Trials. They see many parties of various backgrounds, going from nobility to even the occult. Down their, they met with an eccentric man known as Jaspar Delaware, a pixie cartographer/explorer, who ended up joining the party with the trials.

As the party lined to enter the Underground Halls, where gladiators and parties station themselves before the fight, they were approached by a tiefling man. This tiefling, revealed through this brief encounter, is not a tiefling but a devil who has laid a spell upon Lucky. 

The party is second to participate in the games, after the Ivory Standard (Ilya Tesserson's party, the favorites).

### Session Outline
- Start the session with a mini-recap session and chill. Have players write out 1-2 key motivations for your player to actively work to investigate either by themselves or as a party during downtime for the Winter Trials
	- Tonally, I think this is a time where you have found some comfort but still have a looming pressure around you surrounding the mystery of the situation in this campaign. A bit meta, but important for you guys to know about the way I'm organizing this game. I have two main stories I am opening for exploration: 
		- A:  Sage's Mystery: The mystery behind Sage's Hollow, Alorus, and the devils
		- B: Corruption in the City: Big Chuck's Gravel Rats, the Fang, and the Elite
		- Each of the small mysteries I've laid out have some connection to these two plot points: RRR -> Corruption and Devil Mark -> Sage
		- I have multiple BBEGs prepped as possibilities, so while I do think both should be explored, you do have freedom in choosing which conflict your character would be more invested in
	- This current adventure, the Winter Trials, can be just run as a sort of battle gauntlet, but I think that'd be pretty boring. If that's something you guys are interested in, I can spend more time making more interesting combat and less on the mystery. Personally I want the other plot points are integrated as the main conflict, and the Winter Trials are just a stage where that conflict takes place. 
	- To make things go a bit faster and get better engagement, I want to ask you guys to write down like 1-2 pretty big goals for your characters. This can be on your character sheet, or a notecard on your desk, or whatever. Just make it visible when playing so that if you ever get bored in session or don't know what to do, you can just look at that goal and try to work towards it. If you have a goal, and don't know where to start, you can just ask. "Hey I want to investigate more about the Bloody-Foot, since we know he might be in the city. Where would be character think to start etc..."
		- I'd recommend a clue or plot point that has been brought up before
			- i.e Adelaide wants to know the identity of the Sage, or Atar wants to find the "Birth of the First Spark," since both of these were mentioned in the fortunes provided in Gristwell earlier.
- Party starts in the Underground Halls, inside the small assigned preparation room.
- At some point, [[Chaos]], now hired by some degenerate gamblers controlled by the wealthy, approach with a letter of proposition. This letter will ask the party to resign from the Winter Trials in exchange for a non-insignificant amount of gold. 
	- If the party is at home, Chaos will read the letter aloud
	- If the party is not a home or arrive later in the night, they will receive a letter containing a gold piece each.
