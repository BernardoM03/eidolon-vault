## Recap
Last session, you started in the office of Vurn Orleth, Grand Seneschal of Catameria. Discussing plans regarding the situation, your meeting was interrupted by an older tiefling man, with a tiger-striped cloak and fine clothes, Baeldric Vauldra. His playfulness and ego seemed to offset everyone except for Jaspar, who offered to have a drink sometime. 

Following, you left the office and had lunch with Lucky's dad at a restaurant in the Merial Docks. While enjoying the meal, you planned to find information about what House Meredane was hiding. Lucky and Cogsworth, you snuck onto a boat following the "agent" you met at South-Point Lighthouse. Cogsworth, you continued to tail him and Lucky you waited within the cargo-hold to find out what was being shipped. Cogsworth, you tailed him but not well enough to do so without alerting his attention. After a tense few minutes, you call him over and attempt to cause a distraction to kidnap him. Instead, he starts the barfight and attempts to escape, but gets an ANCHOR dropped on him. You ALL get kicked out, but successfully captured the agent, bringing him home to interrogate more information out of him. 

Last we left off, you all ready for a long-rest decided to sleep at Brennly's House. Just before the time for sleep, some of you could hear the shattering of glass. Lucky and Twila, I believe, you decided to see what was happening outside. The orange light was first apparent, followed by the acrid smell of Alchemist's Fire burning upon your roof. 

Partially illuminated by the flames, were the shining and oily black feathers of Edward Crowley, A Gravel Rat captain and the chief dealer in magical artifacts and contraband. In his annoyingly nasally voice, he issued commands to five other Aarakocra. "Spread fire across the roof. Drive them out into the horde or trap them in with the flames." 

Inside the building, the glass windows shatter inwards as rocks are pelted into the foyer. Jaspar, you see climbing from the window, the familiar brutish arm of [[Renk the Sorry]] as he claims his "master was not very happy with what happened today." And in that moment, he disappeared. 

Please remember the places you last explored with your characters and place them accordingly, and once you are ready, roll initiative.

### Gravel Rat Bandit Groups
Stay within positions. Focus fire anyone who attempts to leave through the windows. They will directly follow orders from Crowley, but disperse once 4/6 members die.
- +4 to hit, n attacks for each rat in group, 1d8+2 piercing
- AC 14, Rat dies if they take more than 6 dmg.
- AoE attacks have potential to over cap damage, killing 1 for each instance of 6 dmg.

### Crowley Murderers
Maintain aerial superiority. Move within sight to attack and immediately leave sight. Hide within the smoke to gain the best obfuscation. 
1. Use up the rest of the alchemists fire on the roof. 
2. Hold position and wait for a target to show. Up to two murderers will group on any individual at a time. Prioritize squishy casters for Grapple and Drop. Otherwise, attack at range
- +6 to hit, 1d6+3 dmg. On crit, DC 13 Con or stunned.
- AC 15, 26 HP each, 50ft flying

### Edward Crowley
Stays airborne and directs his forces with his keen vision and battle tactics. Doesn't attack until it is required of him. He will only engage in melee when the conditions are ideal.
1. Target is able to be taken down in a swift turn OR there is a numerical advantage
2. A target is downed. He will order two murderers to follow him, and will retrieve the body.
Crowley will retreat if he approaches 50% HP. If 3+ of his Murderer's die, he will also call off the mission, but in Auran. He will leave the bandits as a distraction. This will be considered defeat. 
- +9 to hit, 2 attacks with 1d6+6 each. 1x charge for additional 2d6 lightning.
- 3 Charges: 30ft teleport with DC14 DEX dealing 8d6 to those in line
- AC 17, 84 HP, 60ft flying

### Renk the Sorry
Stays invisible as constantly as possible. Will threaten the party to get more information about "the original design." There is a bit of a puzzle in the language barrier here that the party must decipher. Once they know what he wants, the party can negotiate and easily convince him to join their side.
- +9 to hit, 2 attacks with 1d12+6 + 1d8 necrotic claw or 2d6+2 with Frighten ray.
- AC 14, 127 HP, 30ft movement, Invisibility as a bonus action

## Post Combat 
Combat is now over, for the most part. No more initiative, no more Gravel Rats. The enemies have dispersed, but they do see one figure down the street. The halfling figure of Brennly Wriggle.
### Brennly's Return
Brennly, now after his rest and travel back, has made it home to Catameria. He strolls home, only to find his estate has been reduced to rubble. 

He is devastated of course, but does not harbor any ill will to Atar or the party. Instead, he apologizes for his own actions. He cannot say any more, but he hopes that they will forgive him for dragging them into such a wild situation. 

After a few minutes, a few [[Stone Guard]] members appear, and try to get a bit of information out of the party. They don't want the party to leave because they are actually paid off by the Gravel Rats, but they will say "We need to keep you here so we can get the details. You are safe with us." 

You hear more Gravel Rats appear from down the neighborhood. 

The [[Stone Guard]] tells them not to worry, and they should stay here. This is a bluff, and a DC 14 Insight by any party member is enough to tell them that they aren't worried for your safety, but rather worried about having to KEEP you here.
### Finding a Place to Rest
The attack on the colosseum, the attack where you sleep, continuing to chase you down through the night, and even bribing Stone Guard to keep you in place. The Gravel Rats are relentless tonight, but it's been a long day. If you stay up any longer than about an hour more, I am going to ask for some CON saves for exhaustion. What's your plan to find a place to rest.

### Renk and Meredane Manor
If the party made a deal with Renk, they could accompany him to the Stone Crown for the first time, to the Meredane Manor. 

The giant, awkward and erratic in his hand movements, shapes strands of ink and shadow within his fingers. It spreads it across the area, linking  to you all to a Pass Without a Trace. Your footsteps leave no sound or trace, and the light does not cast your shadow. 

You quickly leave Goodbarrel Ave and make your way north, to the Verdure District. As you reach the main gates,  each of you hear Renk's whisper behind you. "Tell the guard you've just come back from a long day of fishing."

The walk through the Verdure District goes by simply. The well-lit streets are extraordinarily clean, the pathways made of ornately carved black slate. The houses here range from large to absurd. Some of the smaller houses contain decorative lanterns, as you would see in the Lantern Ward, but you know these people are performative posers. 

This is quite possibly the nicest place most of you have ever been, especially Adelaide. But Adelaide, as you cross a street, your vision catches a small glimpse of a manor up north this road. This pulls at your mind, just like when you saw that cave in Cofgod, but stronger. There are flashes in your mind. Visions of the Sage's Cairn, all the villagers you know have died. Your sister in particular. 

### Alone
As you walk through the neighborhood, the brass and copper lanterns make shadows long and specific. The only sounds are of the winds, a faint batting of wings, and the occasional thump of the rooftops. There is nowhere to hide here, only places to look like you are walking with purpose. Your cloaks work to hide your general figure, as you occasionally pass by groups of delinquents you believe are searching for you.
