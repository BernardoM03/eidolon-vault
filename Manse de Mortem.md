## Description

Fortuna Mortem
3 Gravel Rat Lieutenants
12 Gravel Rat Enforcers
### Outside 
Statues line the approach to the entrance, depicting heroic figures fighting mythical beasts. All the statues have been defaced to display Fortuna's face.

A small **guard house** sits beside the gate where two or three Gravel Rat enforcers keep watch. The guards spend much of their time drinking, gambling, or observing traffic in Wall Town.
### First Floor
The first floor contains the main social rooms of the mansion. The original architecture is intact, though the Gravel Rats have added barricades and improvised defenses.

**Entry Hall**
A wide foyer with a marble floor and a grand staircase leading to the second level. Expensive rugs now cover parts of the floor. A long table sits against the wall where guards play cards and drink. Weapon racks and spare crossbows are stacked nearby.

**Main Hall**
The largest room in the mansion. Once a reception chamber for parties and noble gatherings, it now serves as the main gathering space for the Gravel Rats. Tables are covered in gambling games, bottles of liquor, and scattered coin. A balcony from the second floor overlooks this room.

**Dining Room**
A massive dining table stretches the length of the room. The Rats use it to divide spoils from recent jobs or hold gang meetings. Chairs are mismatched and many are broken.

**Kitchen**
The kitchen still functions. A cook hired by the gang prepares large communal meals. Barrels of cheap alcohol and sacks of stolen food fill the pantry. A back door leads outside into a narrow service alley.

**Storage Room**
Originally a wine cellar room connected to the dining area. Now it stores stolen goods, weapons, and smuggled cargo waiting to be moved elsewhere in Wall Town.
### Second Floor

**Fortuna Mortem's Master Bedroom**
The largest and most luxurious room in the mansion. The bed is enormous, draped in heavy fabric and surrounded by stolen decorations. Chests of valuables and jewelry sit against the walls. This room is always guarded.

**Guest Bedroom**
Now used by senior members of the Gravel Rats. The room contains several beds and personal belongings belonging to trusted lieutenants.

**Barracks Room**
A former guest chamber converted into sleeping quarters for enforcers. Several bunk beds fill the space, along with armor racks and weapon stands.

**Entertainment Room**
Originally designed as a music or game room. The Gravel Rats use it for gambling, drinking, and private celebrations. Cards, dice, and illicit substances are common here. A hidden trapdoor beneath a rug leads to the basement staircase.

### Basement

**Prison Cells**
Iron bars divide the room into several small holding cells. Prisoners awaiting ransom, interrogation, or execution are kept here. The air smells damp and foul. Chains and manacles hang from the walls.

**Interrogation Chamber**
A stone room fitted with crude torture devices and a heavy wooden chair bolted to the floor. Blood stains mark the walls and drains run along the stone floor. Gravel Rat interrogators bring prisoners here when they need information quickly. A locked cabinet in the corner contains tools and bottles of alchemical stimulants used to keep prisoners conscious.
